Where can I afford to live on $50,000 a year?

On a household income of $50,000, a standard 10%-down scenario supports an estimated home price up to about $154,542 — keeping housing near 28% of gross income.

The assumptions behind this estimate

  • 10% down payment
  • 6.70% interest rate, 30-year fixed
  • Property tax 1.1%/yr, insurance 0.45%/yr, PMI 0.6%/yr of the loan
  • Housing capped near 28% of gross income

At the top of that budget (~$154,542), the estimated payment is about $1,167/month ($898 principal & interest + $142 tax + $58 insurance + $70 PMI).

States within reach on $50,000

No states have a typical (mid-tier) home within that estimated budget. Many still have starter homes in reach — try the income-needed map or the starter-home view.
